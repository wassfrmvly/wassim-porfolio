The Best Movies According to Wassim

My top 3 movies of all-time

The Green Mile

The Green Mile (1999) is a powerful drama based on Stephen King's novel, directed by Frank Darabont. Set in the 1930s, the film tells the story of Paul Edgecomb (played by Tom Hanks), a death row corrections officer at Cold Mountain Penitentiary, and his experiences with a group of inmates.

The movie centers on John Coffey (Michael Clarke Duncan), a gentle giant of a man who has been sentenced to death for the murder of two young girls. Despite his imposing size, Coffey exhibits an extraordinary ability to heal people and perform miracles, leading Paul to question his guilt. Coffey's supernatural powers and his simple, kind nature deeply affect the lives of those around him, including Paul's fellow guards and the other inmates on death row, known as "The Green Mile."

Throughout the film, Paul's growing realization of Coffey's innocence conflicts with the cruel reality of the justice system. The narrative explores themes of morality, justice, the death penalty, and the possibility of redemption. As the story unfolds, Paul's struggle with his conscience becomes the emotional core of the film.

The Green Mile is a touching, thought-provoking movie that deals with the complexities of human nature and the consequences of both good and evil.

The Shawshank Redemption

The Shawshank Redemption (1994), directed by Frank Darabont and based on a Stephen King novella, is an inspiring drama about hope, friendship, and perseverance. The film follows the story of Andy Dufresne (played by Tim Robbins), a banker who is wrongfully convicted of murdering his wife and her lover. Sentenced to life imprisonment at Shawshank State Penitentiary, Andy faces the brutal reality of life behind bars.

Inside the prison, Andy befriends Ellis "Red" Redding (played by Morgan Freeman), a long-time inmate who is known for smuggling contraband. Despite the harsh conditions, Andy maintains his dignity and refuses to be broken by the system. His intelligence and financial skills catch the attention of the corrupt warden and prison guards, leading him to manage the prison's finances and launder money for the warden's illegal schemes.

Over the years, Andy becomes a source of hope and inspiration for his fellow inmates, improving the prison library and helping others pursue education. Despite the seemingly hopeless situation, Andy harbors a secret plan to escape Shawshank and reclaim his life. The film's climax reveals Andy's meticulous and long-prepared escape, exposing the warden's corruption in the process.

Red, who has always viewed hope as dangerous in prison, ultimately finds his own redemption through Andy's influence. The film ends on a hopeful note, with Red finally earning his parole and reuniting with Andy in freedom.

"The Shawshank Redemption" explores themes of friendship, resilience, and the power of hope, making it a timeless and emotionally resonant story.

Non-stop

Non-Stop (2014) is an action-thriller directed by Jaume Collet-Serra and starring Liam Neeson as Bill Marks, a troubled U.S. federal air marshal. The film takes place almost entirely aboard a transatlantic flight from New York to London. Marks, battling personal demons and alcoholism, boards the flight with the intention of performing his routine duty. However, things take a dangerous turn when he receives a series of mysterious text messages from an unknown passenger.

The messages demand that $150 million be transferred to a specific account, or a passenger on the plane will be killed every 20 minutes. As the deadline approaches and passengers begin to die, Marks finds himself in a race against time to identify the terrorist on board. To make matters worse, the situation is manipulated to make Marks look like the hijacker, turning the passengers, crew, and law enforcement against him.

As tensions rise and paranoia spreads, Marks must navigate the chaos and outsmart the real perpetrator. Along the way, he is aided by flight attendant Nancy (Michelle Dockery) and fellow passenger Jen Summers (Julianne Moore), as they work together to uncover the truth.

In the film's climax, it is revealed that the mastermind behind the plot is a former soldier who wants to expose the vulnerability of air travel and the failures of national security post-9/11. Marks ultimately saves the plane and its passengers in a high-stakes final showdown, restoring his reputation in the process.
